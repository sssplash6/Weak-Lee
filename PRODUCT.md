# Product

## Register

product

## Users
The freshman.academy team: a small group of colleagues who set weekly and monthly goals, break them into subtasks, and hold each other accountable (missed submissions and skipped meetings incur small fines). They open the dashboard a few times a week on desktop, usually mid-task, to update progress or check on teammates.

## Product Purpose
A shared accountability tracker. Each member submits goals per week/month, tracks completion honestly (percent-based, subtask-driven), reflects on unfinished goals before starting a new period, and sees the team's directory and archive. Success: goals get written down, progress stays honest, and closing a period forces reflection.

## Brand Personality
Calm, honest, minimal. The interface should feel like a well-kept notebook: quiet surfaces, clear numbers, no gamification or celebration theatrics.

## Anti-references
- Heavy SaaS dashboards: stat-tile grids, gradients, badge confetti.
- Habit-tracker apps with streaks, mascots, and reward animations.
- Anything that reads bulky or clunky: oversized pills, stacked bordered containers, competing rounded boxes.

## Design Principles
- One accent, one meaning: navy = actions/selection, orange = progress, red = destructive. Color never decorates.
- Prefer familiar product patterns (tabs, inline rows, plain lists) over invented containers; not everything needs a card.
- Density with calm: compact controls, generous whitespace between sections, hairline borders over shadows.
- Honesty over polish: progress numbers, timestamps, and fines are shown plainly, never softened.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No formal WCAG target set. Keep text ≥ 14px, maintain AA-ish contrast on text, keyboard-reachable controls, and aria labels on icon-only affordances (existing convention: progress ring and pickers carry aria-labels).
